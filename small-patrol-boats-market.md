<p><p>The small patrol boats market is divided into two primary segments: Inshore Patrol Vessels (IPVs) and Offshore Patrol Vessels (OPVs). IPV refers to vessels designed for operations closer to shore, focusing on coastal security, fisheries protection, and law enforcement in shallow waters. In contrast, OPVs are built for extended missions further offshore, engaging in maritime surveillance, anti-piracy, and border control. Both types emphasize versatility and efficiency, catering to naval forces and coast guard operations for enhanced maritime security.</p></p>
