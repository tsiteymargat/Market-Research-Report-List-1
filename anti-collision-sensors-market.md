<p><p>The anti-collision sensors market primarily consists of infrared and laser sensors. Infrared sensors use light waves to detect obstacles by measuring the reflection of emitted infrared light, making them suitable for short-range applications, such as in vehicles and robotics. Laser sensors, on the other hand, utilize laser beams for precise distance measurement and are effective in various environments. They offer greater accuracy over longer distances, benefiting industries like automation, automotive, and security. Both types enhance safety and efficiency in collision detection systems.</p></p>
